## Executive Summary

This Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) report provides a structured profile of the hacktivist group **H3C4KEDZ**, an entity operating from Cambodia and linked to the broader **AnonSec-KH** network. The group is known for targeting Thai infrastructure and educational institutions, including a reported breach of Mahidol University with **over 1.5 TB of exfiltrated data**.

The analysis is based on:

- Network graph data from the `stealth mole` tool
- Telegram channel activity from `We_H3c4kedz1`
- Publicly available scrape results and related indicators

The threat presents a credible risk to Thai government, education, and technology sectors, especially where sensitive data and exposed infrastructure overlap.

## Methodology

This report uses OSINT principles to triangulate multiple data sources:

- **Network graphs** from the `stealth mole` platform
- **Telegram channel metadata** and message logs
- **Image evidence** captured from intelligence dashboards and channel posts
- **Timeline correlation** from August 2025 reporting

The goal is to create a repeatable visibility model for this actor’s tactics, capabilities, and target selection.

## Threat Actor Profile

H3C4KEDZ operates as a Cambodia-based hacktivist collective allied with **AnonSec-KH**. Their activity is motivated by the Cambodia-Thailand border conflict and appears to be primarily disruptive rather than financially motivated.

Key attributes:

- **Primary target region:** Thailand
- **Affiliation:** AnonSec-KH / Cambodian hacktivist network
- **Reported activity:** Data exfiltration, website defacement, intelligence leakage
- **Evidence sources:** Telegram channel posts and network graph analysis

## Activities and Operations

H3C4KEDZ exhibits a range of activities consistent with a motivated hacktivist cell:

- **High-profile breaches:** Mahidol University exfiltration of academic and administrative data
- **Public channel reporting:** Telegram posts referencing campaign milestones and leaked material
- **Reconnaissance and targeting:** Use of network graphing to map vulnerable Thai infrastructure

![Network evidence from channel posts](/assets/img/osint/fb4268bc-5e5b-44d1-b69d-12f387e5472f.png)

## Key Findings

- The group uses publicly observable communication channels to claim stolen data and coordinate activity.
- Their operational focus is primarily educational and governmental targets in Thailand.
- The reporting suggests both data theft and reputational disruption are part of their mission.

## Recommendations for Defenders

1. **Monitor relevant Telegram channels and threat feeds** for emerging TTPs.
2. **Harden exposed educational systems** and apply least-privilege controls to research and admin networks.
3. **Prioritize response for any indicators linked to Mahidol University or AnonSec-KH infrastructure.**

## Conclusion

H3C4KEDZ represents a focused regional hacktivist threat with demonstrable targeting of Thai infrastructure. OSINT collection on this actor should remain active, especially around Telegram channels, leaked data repositories, and network graph indicators.
